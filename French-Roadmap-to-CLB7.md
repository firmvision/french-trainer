## The honest timeline

The target for Canadian immigration is **CLB/NCLC 7 in all four skills** (reading, writing, listening, speaking), proven on the **TEF Canada** or **TCF Canada** test. CLB 7 is roughly **B2** on the European scale — upper-intermediate, not fluent.

Going from A2 to B2 takes most adult learners **~350–400 focused hours**. At 1–2 hours a day (call it ~1.5 average), that is about **6–9 months of consistent work**, not 3.

So the plan below treats **3 months as Phase 1** — getting you firmly to **B1 and a working daily habit** — and then carries you through to the test. If at any point you can raise your daily hours to 3–4, the back half compresses sharply.

| Phase | Months | Target level | Approx. hours |
|-------|--------|--------------|---------------|
| Phase 1 — Foundation | Months 1–3 | A2 → solid B1 | ~135–180 |
| Phase 2 — Build to B2 | Months 4–6 | B1 → B2 / CLB 7 | ~135–180 |
| Phase 3 — Test sprint | Months 7–8 | Lock in CLB 7, sit TEF | ~90–120 |

Realistic test date at 1–2 hrs/day: **early 2027**. At 3–4 hrs/day: **late 2026**.

## What CLB 7 actually requires (TEF Canada minimums)

You must hit the minimum in **every** section — you cannot average a weak skill against a strong one.

| Skill | TEF Canada score for CLB 7 |
|-------|----------------------------|
| Reading | 434 / 699 |
| Writing | 428 / 699 |
| Listening | 434 / 699 |
| Speaking | 456 / 699 |

Speaking has the highest bar, so conversation practice matters more than grammar drilling. Note: **TEF/TCF results are valid 2 years**, so don't book the test until you're genuinely close.

## Phase 1 (Months 1–3): Foundation — your 3-month focus

Goal: move from A2 to a confident B1, fix pronunciation early, and make daily French automatic.

**Outcomes by end of Month 3:**
- Present, past (passé composé + imparfait), and near-future tenses solid
- ~1,500 active vocabulary words
- Can follow slow-to-normal French audio and hold a 5-minute conversation
- A daily routine you don't have to think about

### Daily routine (1–2 hours)

Split your time so all four skills get touched every week. A sample day:

- **20–30 min — Core course / grammar** (Phase 1 driver below)
- **15–20 min — Listening** (podcast or slowed video, active)
- **15 min — Vocabulary** (spaced repetition / Anki)
- **15–20 min — Speaking or writing** (shadowing, journaling, or a tutor session)

On 2-hour days, double the listening and speaking blocks — those are your slowest-growing skills and the test's hardest sections.

### Recommended resources for Phase 1

- **Core course:** *Duolingo* (streak + base) **plus** a structured method — *Coffee Break French* (Seasons 1–2) or *Français Authentique* for habit and grammar logic.
- **Grammar reference:** *Le Conjugueur* (verbs), *Lawless French* (free, excellent explanations), or the *Bescherelle* app.
- **Listening:** *InnerFrench* podcast (made for intermediate learners), *News in Slow French*, *TV5Monde Apprendre* (free, leveled A1–B2).
- **Vocabulary:** *Anki* with a French frequency deck — 10–15 new cards/day.
- **Speaking (start in Week 3–4):** *italki* or *Preply* tutor, 1–2 sessions/week. This single habit moves your speaking score more than anything else.

## Phase 2 (Months 4–6): Build to B2 / CLB 7

Goal: cross from B1 into B2 — the level the test actually measures.

- Shift listening to **native-speed** content (TV5Monde news, *Hugo Décrypte* on YouTube, French Netflix with French subtitles).
- Start **writing structured pieces**: opinion paragraphs, short formal letters — these mirror the TEF writing tasks (Section A: continue a news story; Section B: argue a position).
- Increase tutor sessions to **2–3/week**, focused on argued opinions and describing/comparing — the TEF speaking format.
- Vocabulary shifts from survival words to **abstract/argumentative** language (cause, consequence, opinion, nuance).

---

## Phase 3 (Months 7–8): Test sprint

Goal: convert B2 ability into the exact scores, then sit the test.

- Buy **TEF Canada prep materials**: *PrepMyFrench*, *Le Robert TEF*, or the official CCI Paris IDF samples. Do **full timed mock tests** weekly.
- Drill the **four exact formats** until they're reflexes — especially Speaking (456 is the hardest minimum) and Writing.
- Book your **TEF Canada** test only once mocks consistently clear the CLB 7 thresholds with margin.
- Find an official test center near you and register ~4–6 weeks ahead.

## How French changes your Express Entry profile

This is why the effort pays off:

- CLB 7 French + at least CLB 5 English = **up to 50 extra CRS points**.
- It unlocks **French-language category draws**, which have been dramatically lower than general draws — **Draw 405 (March 2026) cleared at CRS 393**, vs 515+ for general rounds.
- Canada is targeting **10% French-speaking newcomers outside Quebec by 2027**, so these draws are frequent and protected policy.

For someone at a mid-range CRS, French is often the difference between waiting indefinitely and getting an invitation.
